Argue the side you didn't choose.

In a real debate, the easiest thing to do is knock down a weak, cartoon version of the other side. That's a straw man — and it convinces no one who actually disagrees with you. The harder, more valuable move is to build the steelman: the strongest, fairest version of the opposing case.

🥀 Straw man
You attack the weakest, silliest version of the other side. Easy — but it dodges the real argument.
🛡️ Steelman
You build the other side's best case, as they'd make it. Harder — but it's the only one worth answering.
Why bother? Three reasons: you can't rebut an argument you don't understand; you spot weak reasoning faster once you know what strong reasoning looks like; and you disagree more fairly — which is exactly what a good Assembly debate, and an impartial chamber, depends on.

You'll be handed a motion and told which side to argue — sometimes the side you disagree with. Your job isn't to win. It's to build a case the other side would recognise as fair. Pick a motion to begin.

A Youth Assembly skill-builder. Every motion can be argued either way — the game scores the quality of your reasoning, never which side is “right”.
