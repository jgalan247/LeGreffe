Order! Order!
Ruling 1 of 6

You're in the Chair. Keep the Assembly in order.

Every States sitting is run by a presiding officer — the Bailiff, or in their absence whoever is presiding. They decide who speaks, rule on the rules, keep Members to the point, and call the votes — but they do not take sides and cannot vote themselves. Take the Chair for one sitting. Six moments will test your judgement.

Your job: apply the Standing Orders fairly. Rule well and the debate runs cleanly; rule poorly and order — and the Assembly's respect for the Chair — starts to slip.
