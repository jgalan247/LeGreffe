You're the Minister
Year 1 of 3

A term in office. Three Budgets. One Assembly to keep on side.

You're a Minister for a three-year term. Each year you propose a Budget from a limited pot, a Scrutiny Panel reviews it, the States Assembly amends and votes, and your choices ripple into the years that follow. Neglect something and it comes back to bite. Spread yourself too thin and the Assembly won't back you.

Each year: share out the money → face the Scrutiny Panel → the Assembly amends & votes (25 of 49 needed) → live with the consequences next year.

Most Members are independents and no party holds a majority, so you have no guaranteed votes — every Budget must be negotiated with individual Members to win a majority.

Figures illustrative, based on Jersey's 2026 Budget (total net spending ~£1,278m; Health £381m, Education £188m, Housing & Social Security £113m).

The Assembly debates

Most Members of the Assembly are independents, and no party holds a majority — so you have no guaranteed bloc of votes. You must build a majority Member by Member. Each amendment is a Member asking for something in return for support. Accept it (compromise — wins votes, but moves your money) or resist it (defend your plan, and risk the vote).

A learning game about trade-offs over time and how the States Assembly scrutinises, amends and approves a Budget. No allocation is "right" — every choice has a cost and a benefit, and no Minister governs alone.
